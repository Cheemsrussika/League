Notes

  • Awe grants a total of 10 bonus attack damage by itself without any stacks of Manaflow.
    • With Manamune's attack damage stat this totals to 45.
  • Empowered basic attacks gained from abilities will count for triggering Manaflow upon consuming the attack.
  • The user's current mana is increased the same amount as their maximum mana from consuming charges of Manaflow.
  • Manaless champions cannot trigger Manaflow.
  • Selling a Manaflow item removes the bonus mana gained from the effect but retains the stacks if these items are bought again, regardless of which item generated the stacks.

Arena differences edit
  • Attack damage changed to 40.
  • Mana changed to 600.
  • New Unique Passive - Mana Charge: After 2 combat rounds from the current round, this item transforms into Muramana.
  • Removed Unique Passive - Manaflow: Occasionally grants a charge, and consumes a charge on-hit and whenever affecting an enemy or ally with an ability to grant bonus mana, increased against champions, up to a maximum.

Trivia

  • Assuming a charge is gained every 8 seconds, the 360 mana charge maxing takes 16 minutes, reduced to 8 minutes if hitting champions.
  • 'Manamune' references Masamune.
    • By extension, it might be referencing Sephiroth's sword in Final Fantasy VII, whose name itself is already a reference.
  • Its appearance is very reminiscent of the titular weapon from Soulcalibur IV.
  • The Quick Charge version was also used on the Crystal Scar during Rotating Game Modes.
  • Featured in the Journal of Justice (Issue 28: Alowicious Chucat Dies in Magical Fire).
